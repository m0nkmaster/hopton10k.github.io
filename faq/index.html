Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions about entering the Hopton 10k? Find answers below. For race day specific information (parking, check-in, route details), see our Race Day page.

About the Race

When is the race?

Sunday 27th September 2026 at 10am

The Hopton 10k takes place on the first Sunday in October (or late September) every year.

Where does the race start?

The race starts and finishes at Hopton Primary School, Woodend Road, Mirfield, WF14 8PR. From there, the route heads through Lower Hopton before entering the beautiful woodland trails.

What’s the route like?

The 10k route offers fantastic variety: quiet country roads, woodland trails, bridleways, and footpaths. Expect some short but challenging climbs and stunning views over the Calder Valley. Given the time of year, there may be a few muddy patches!

Who can enter?

You must be 15 years of age or older to enter the Hopton 10k.

Entering the Race

How do I enter?

Enter online via our RaceBest registration page. You’ll receive a confirmation email once your entry is complete.

When do entries close?

Entries close at midday on the Saturday before the race. We cannot accept entries on race day.

Can I transfer my entry to someone else?

Contact us at info@hopton10k.run at least 24 hours before the race and we can arrange a transfer or refund at our discretion. Refunds are subject to a £2.50 admin charge. No refunds or transfers on race day.

How do I know my entry has been received?

You should receive a confirmation email from RaceBest. If you haven’t received one, email info@hopton10k.run.

What’s Included

What do I get for my entry?

  • A fantastic, well-organised race through varied terrain
  • A technical running T-shirt for all finishers
  • Water station at the 5k point and at the finish
  • Chip timing and official results
  • A goody bag with treats at the finish

Are there prizes?

We don’t award prizes, but completing the Hopton 10k is its own reward! There will be sweet treats waiting for you at the finish.

The Cause

Where does the money go?

All proceeds from the Hopton 10k go directly to supporting Hopton Primary School. The event is entirely organised and run by volunteers.

Can I raise money for charity?

Absolutely! We’d be delighted if you’d consider raising sponsorship for Hopton Primary School, but you’re welcome to run for any cause close to your heart.

Race Rules

Can I walk the race?

The Hopton 10k is a running race licensed by UK Athletics. Participants should make best efforts to complete the course within the 90-minute cut-off. Marshals will start packing up at 11:30am.

Can I run with a buggy or wheelchair?

Unfortunately, the terrain (woodland trails, stiles, uneven ground) cannot accommodate buggies or wheelchairs.

Can I run with my dog?

No. Under UK Athletics rules, dogs (apart from medical assistance dogs) are not permitted in licensed competitions.

More Questions?
